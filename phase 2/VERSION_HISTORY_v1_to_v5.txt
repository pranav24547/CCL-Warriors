  VERSION 1.0 — THE FOUNDATION (Phase 1 Baseline)
  Author: Pranav  |  Products: 30  |  Data: Phase 1 Excel

WHAT IT WAS:
  The first version built from scratch for the Phase 1 competition.
  A classic 6-step demand planning pipeline following Cisco's own methodology
  guidance. This was a "by-the-book" implementation.

HOW IT WORKED:

  Step 1: Weighted Moving Average (WMA)
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  - Used last 4 quarters of actual booking data
  - Weights: [1, 2, 3, 4] / 10 (40% weight on most recent quarter)
  - This gives more importance to recent data while not ignoring older trends
  - Example: If last 4 quarters were [1000, 1200, 900, 1100]
             WMA = (1000×1 + 1200×2 + 900×3 + 1100×4) / 10 = 1090

  Step 2: Big Deal Cleaning
  ─────────────────────────
  - If big deals > 10% of total bookings in any quarter → flag that product
  - For flagged products: use "Average Deals" (excluding big deals) as the
    baseline for WMA instead of raw actuals
  - Add back 50% of the average historical big deal amount
  - WHY: Big deals create artificial spikes. If one quarter had a massive
    one-time government order, WMA would be inflated. Cleaning removes this.

  Step 3: Expert Ensemble
  ───────────────────────
  - Three expert forecasts available: Demand Planner (DP), Marketing (MK),
    Data Science (DS)
  - Simple bias correction: corrected = forecast / (1 + avg_bias)
  - Outlier removal: if one expert forecasts > 2× another, drop the outlier
  - Final ensemble = simple average of remaining forecasts
  - Example: DP=1000, MK=950, DS=3500 → DS is >2× MK → drop DS
             Ensemble = (1000 + 950) / 2 = 975

  Step 4: Lifecycle-Based 3-Component Blending
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────
  - Each product has a lifecycle stage: Sustaining, Decline, NPI-Ramp
  - Different stages get different blend weights:
      Sustaining:  25% WMA + 25% Trend + 50% Expert Ensemble
      Decline:     40% WMA + 30% Trend + 30% Ensemble
      NPI-Ramp:    10% WMA +  5% Trend + 85% Ensemble (trust experts most)
  - WHY: Declining products need more statistical grounding (WMA/trend).
    New products have little history, so experts matter more.

  Step 5: Q2 Seasonal Index
  ─────────────────────────
  - seasonal_idx = avg(Q2 values) / avg(all quarters)
  - Bounded between [0.70, 1.40] to prevent extreme adjustments
  - Applied as a multiplier: forecast × seasonal_idx
  - Example: If Q2 historically runs 20% above average → multiply by 1.20

  Step 6: Sanity Checks
  ─────────────────────
  - Asymmetric loss penalty: for uncertain high-cost products, shade 5-8% below
  - SCMS/VMS segment trend adjustments (±3-5%)
  - QoQ bounds: flag if forecast > ±30% from last actual

STRENGTHS:
  - Clean, systematic pipeline that follows established methodology
  - Used ALL available data: actuals, experts, big deals, SCMS, VMS
  - Lifecycle-aware blending

WEAKNESSES (discovered later):
  - Expert weighting was naive (simple average, not accuracy-weighted)
  - Bias correction was simplistic: divided by (1+bias) instead of
    subtracting proportional bias
  - Seasonal index was a simple ratio, not recency-weighted
  - No per-product tuning — same rules for all 30 products
  - No backtesting framework to validate accuracy

RESULT: ~77% accuracy in Phase 1 competition (last place)

WHAT CHANGED FROM v1.0:

  Change 1: Accuracy²-Weighted Expert Ensemble
  ─────────────────────────────────────────────
  - v1.0 used simple average of expert forecasts
  - v2.0 weights each expert by their accuracy SQUARED
  - Formula: weight_i = accuracy_i² / sum(all accuracy_i²)
  - WHY: Squaring amplifies the difference between good and bad experts.
    An expert with 90% acc gets weight 0.81, while 60% acc gets 0.36 — 
    the better expert gets 2.25× more influence instead of just 1.5×
  - Holdout test: Reduced ensemble MAPE from 20.9% to 18.5% (2.4pp improvement)

  Change 2: Damped Trend (Gardner-McKenzie 1985)
  ───────────────────────────────────────────────
  - v1.0 used raw linear trend extrapolation (y = a + bx)
  - Problem: volatile products would get extreme trend forecasts
  - v2.0 applies a damping factor: phi = max(0.80, min(0.98, 1.0 - CV × 0.30))
    where CV = coefficient of variation of the actual data
  - High volatility → lower phi → more damping → pulls trend toward mean
  - Holdout test: Reduced trend MAPE from 42.7% to 34.1% (8.6pp improvement!)
  - WHY: The more erratic a product's history, the less we should trust
    pure trend extrapolation. A product bouncing between 500 and 5000
    shouldn't be forecasted at 10,000 just because the trend line points up.

  Change 3: Recency-Weighted Seasonal Index
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  - v1.0: seasonal_idx = avg(all Q2 values) / avg(all quarters)  [equal weight]
  - v2.0: FY25Q2 gets 60% weight, FY24Q2 gets 30%, FY23Q2 gets 10%
  - WHY: Recent seasonality is more relevant. If Q2 has been getting stronger
    over time, older Q2 values drag the index down. Recency weighting
    captures the current seasonal pattern.

STRENGTHS:
  - All changes were backed by holdout testing (not intuition)
  - Damped trend dramatically reduced forecast error on volatile products
  - acc² weighting gave better experts more influence

WEAKNESSES:
  - Still had Phase 1's 30-product scope (became 20 in Phase 2)
  - Bias correction still simplistic
  - No structural independence analysis
  - Expert accuracy measured across only 3 quarters (noisy estimates)

RESULT: Improved over v1.0 but still building the foundation for Phase 2

WHAT CHANGED FROM v2.0:

  This was a COMPLETE REWRITE for the Phase 2 competition. The Phase 2 dataset
  had different product scope (20 products instead of 30) and new data sheets.

  Change 1: Expert-Anchored Architecture
  ───────────────────────────────────────
  - v2.0: WMA+Trend+Ensemble blend with lifecycle weights
  - v3.0: TWO-LAYER architecture:
      Layer 1: Expert Anchor (weighted blend of DP, MK, DS)
      Layer 2: Structural Signals (independent forecasting methods)
      Final = expert_weight × Anchor + (1-expert_weight) × Structural
  - WHY: The Phase 1 pipeline treated experts as one of three equal inputs.
    But analysis showed experts consistently outperform pure statistical
    methods for most products. Making experts the PRIMARY anchor and using
    structural signals as guardrails is more accurate.

  Change 2: Seven Structural Signals
  ───────────────────────────────────
  - v2.0 used only WMA and trend as statistical inputs
  - v3.0 computed SEVEN independent signals:
      1. Q2/Q1 ratio × FY26Q1  (seasonal ratio)
      2. YoY Q2 growth × FY25Q2  (year-over-year Q2 trend)
      3. Q2 weighted average (50/35/15% across 3 Q2 quarters)
      4. MA4 (simple average of last 4 quarters)
      5. Big Deal Q2 forecast (decomposed big + average deals)
      6. SCMS bottom-up (sum of per-channel Q2 forecasts)
      7. VMS bottom-up (sum of per-vertical Q2 forecasts)
  - Structural composite = MEDIAN of all signals (robust to outliers)

  Change 3: Granular Expert Weighting Tiers
  ──────────────────────────────────────────
  - v2.0: Fixed lifecycle-based weights
  - v3.0: Expert weight determined by average expert accuracy:
      ≥85% acc → 82% expert / 18% structural
      ≥75% acc → 72% expert / 28% structural
      ≥65% acc → 65% expert / 35% structural
      ≥50% acc → 55% expert / 45% structural
      ≥30% acc → 40% expert / 60% structural
      <30% acc → 25% expert / 75% structural
  - WHY: When experts are very good, trust them more. When they're terrible,
    lean on structural signals instead. This adapts per product.

  Change 4: Consistency-Gated Bias Correction
  ────────────────────────────────────────────
  - v2.0: Simple corrected = forecast / (1 + avg_bias)
  - v3.0: Check if bias is CONSISTENT across quarters:
      If bias direction is the same in 2+ of 3 quarters → correct by 50%
      If bias direction oscillates → correct by only 20%
      If bias is near-zero (<3%) → no correction
  - WHY: An expert who consistently over-predicts by 20% has a systematic
    issue worth correcting. An expert whose bias flips between +15% and
    -10% has random error, not systematic bias — correction would add noise.

  Change 5: Garbage Expert Exclusion
  ──────────────────────────────────
  - If an expert has <5% accuracy or forecasts 0, they're excluded entirely
  - Their forecast doesn't enter the weighted blend at all
  - Applied to: MK on Product #19 (0% acc, forecast 5000),
                MK on Product #20 (0 forecast),
                DP on Product #19 (3% acc)

RESULT: Total forecast ~72,530 units across 20 products

WHAT CHANGED FROM v3.0:

  Change 1: Proper SCMS/VMS Q2 Bottom-Up
  ───────────────────────────────────────
  - v3.0 used FY26Q1 totals for SCMS/VMS bottom-up (WRONG — these are Q1
    values, not Q2 values)
  - v4.0 extracts Q2-SPECIFIC segment history (quarter indices 1, 5, 9 in
    the 13-quarter SCMS/VMS arrays correspond to Q2 quarters)
  - Applies recency weighting to Q2 history: 50/35/15%
  - WHY: Using Q1 data to forecast Q2 ignores seasonality within segments.
    Some channels (e.g., ENTERPRISE) spike in Q2 due to budget flush cycles.

  Change 2: Product-Specific Overrides
  ─────────────────────────────────────
  - v3.0 treated all products with the same framework
  - v4.0 added surgical overrides for 5 pathological products:
    
    Product #1 (WiFi AP): Q2 seasonal spike product
      - Experts forecast 2200-3200, but Q2 history shows 2284→6651→8293
      - Experts are forecasting Q1-level demand, missing the Q2 spike
      - Override: reduce expert weight to 40%, lean on structural signals
    
    Product #9 (IP Phone Desk_2): Q2 is stable despite overall volatility
      - Q2 history: 8791, 6184, 7891 (relatively stable ~7600 avg)
      - But non-Q2 quarters are wildly erratic (29K, 33K, 30K)
      - Override: blend expert anchor with Q2-specific average
    
    Product #11 (Switch HPPoE): All experts terrible (<62% accuracy)
      - Relied on structural signals instead (35% expert / 65% structural)
    
    Product #19 (Router 4P PoE): Only DS is valid (71% acc)
      - DP at 3% acc and MK at 0% acc → both EXCLUDED
      - DS-only anchor with 55% expert weight
    
    Product #20 (Router LTE Wireless): MK excluded (0 forecast)
      - Only DP and DS used, evenly weighted (50/50 blend)

  Change 3: Tightened Sanity Bounds
  ─────────────────────────────────
  - v3.0: Loose bounds of ±80% from last actual
  - v4.0: ±60% lower / ±125% upper from credible consensus range
  - Credible range = [min(all valid expert forecasts, recent actuals),
                      max(all valid expert forecasts, recent actuals)]

NOTABLE v3.0 → v4.0 PRODUCT CHANGES:
  #1  WiFi AP:        4,646 → 5,505  (+18.5%)  [Q2 spike override]
  #9  Phone Desk_2:   8,786 → 8,300  (-5.5%)   [Q2 stable override]
  #20 Router LTE:     1,554 → 1,690  (+8.8%)   [MK exclusion fix]

RESULT: Total forecast 73,226 units (+696 from v3.0, +1.0%)

  VERSION 4.0 → 5.0: THE FORENSIC AUDIT
████████████████████████████████████████████████████████████████████████████████

Before building v5.0, we ran a DEEP FORENSIC AUDIT of v4.0. This involved:

  1. Deep Analysis Script (deep_analysis.py):
     - Computed all raw data, expert correlations, Q2/Q1 ratios, YoY rates,
       big deal decomposition, SCMS/VMS channel trends for all 20 products
     - Generated 70,000+ characters of analysis in deep_analysis_full.txt

  2. Forensic Audit Script (forensic_audit.py):
     - Backtested all forecasting methods against known FY25Q2 actuals
     - For each product, compared: ratio FC, YoY FC, MA4, Big Deal FC,
       SCMS BU, VMS BU against what actually happened
     - Identified which methods work for which products and which are garbage

  3. Line-by-Line Output Audit:
     - Manually traced through every product's forecast card in the v4.0 output
     - Checked: Is the anchor reasonable? Is the structural median sensible?
     - Flagged every case where the math produced a nonsensical result

THIS AUDIT FOUND 8 CRITICAL FLAWS IN v4.0 (detailed below)

EIGHT FLAWS FOUND AND FIXED:

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════
FLAW #1 [CATASTROPHIC] — Product #4 was over-forecasted by ~60%
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  THE PROBLEM:
  v4.0 forecasted 15,239 for IP Phone Desk_1.
  But Demand Planner (DP) has 87.7% accuracy with -0.8% bias (essentially
  PERFECT) and forecasts only 9,500.

  HOW IT HAPPENED:
  - v4.0 used acc² weighting to blend experts:
      DP (88% acc): weight = 0.877² = 0.769  →  9,500 × 0.769 = 7,306
      MK (71% acc): weight = 0.706² = 0.498  → 14,193 × 0.498 = 7,068
      DS (68% acc): weight = 0.681² = 0.464  → 18,984 × 0.464 = 8,809
  - Expert anchor = (7,306 + 7,068 + 8,809) / (0.769+0.498+0.464) = 13,391
  - DS at 68% accuracy with an ABSURD 22,593 forecast (bias-corrected to
    18,984) dragged the anchor from 9,500 all the way up to 13,391
  - Then structural median at 18,010 pushed it up further
  - Final: 13,391 × 0.60 + 18,010 × 0.40 = 15,239

  THE FIX (v5.0):
  Two new mechanisms:
  
  a) DOMINANT EXPERT RULE: If the best expert has ≥82% accuracy AND is
     ≥8% better than the second-best expert:
       Anchor = 80% best expert + 20% acc³-weighted blend of others
     For Product #4: DP (88%) vs MK (71%) — gap is 17%, rule triggers
       Anchor = 9,500 × 0.80 + others × 0.20 = ~10,891
  
  b) acc³ WEIGHTING (instead of acc²): DP gets 0.675 vs DS gets 0.316
     More aggressive penalization of bad experts
  
  RESULT: v4.0 = 15,239 → v5.0 = 11,432 (−25%)

FLAW #2 [CRITICAL] — Five of seven structural signals were CORRELATED
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  THE PROBLEM:
  v4.0 used 7 structural signals and took their MEDIAN. But 5 of them
  were derived from the SAME underlying Q2 actual data:

  Signal               Source Data               Independent?
  ────────────────────  ────────────────────────  ───────────
  Q2/Q1 ratio FC       Q2 actuals + Q1 actuals   YES
  YoY Q2 growth FC     Q2-to-Q2 growth rate      YES
  Q2 weighted average  Q2 actuals directly        NO (= raw Q2 data)
  MA4                  Last 4 quarters            PARTIALLY
  Big Deal Q2 FC       Q2 big + avg = Q2 actual   NO (= Q2 total)
  SCMS Q2 BU           Q2 channel sums = Q2 tot   NO (= Q2 total)
  VMS Q2 BU            Q2 vertical sums = Q2 tot  NO (= Q2 total)

  The Big Deal FC, SCMS BU, and VMS BU are literally just different
  DECOMPOSITIONS of the same Q2 actual number. Adding them all to the
  median means counting the same information 4 times!

  Example (Product #8, IP Video Desk):
    Q2 Wt Avg = 14,834  (from Q2 actuals 52058, 12867, 5044)
    SCMS BU   = 14,834  (same data, summed by channel)
    VMS BU    = 14,844  (same data, summed by vertical)
    These three are essentially IDENTICAL numbers. Having all three in
    the median makes the median = Q2 weighted average no matter what.

  THE FIX (v5.0):
  Removed Q2 weighted average, Big Deal FC, SCMS BU, and VMS BU from
  the structural median. Only 3 truly independent signals remain:
    1. Q2/Q1 Ratio FC  (uses current Q1 as base)
    2. YoY Q2 Growth FC  (uses Q2-to-Q2 trend)
    3. MA4  (partially independent, uses all 4 recent quarters)

  SCMS/VMS are now "validation signals" — displayed for reference but
  NOT used in the structural median calculation.

FLAW #3 [CRITICAL] — Product #2 forecasted BELOW its own most recent quarter
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  THE PROBLEM:
  Switch Enterprise 8-Port PoE+ with Fiber Uplink is a GROWTH product.
  FY26Q1 actual = 5,815 (higher than FY25Q2 = 4,290 — growth confirmed!)
  But v4.0 forecasted only 4,641.

  HOW IT HAPPENED:
  - Structural median = 2,665 (dragged down by zero Q2 history in
    SCMS/VMS — this product barely existed in earlier Q2 periods)
  - 65% expert (5,704) + 35% structural (2,665) = 4,641
  - The structural component pulled the forecast below FY26Q1

  THE FIX (v5.0):
  GROWTH PRODUCT FLOOR: If FY26Q1 > FY25Q2 (growth confirmed):
    - Structural floor = FY26Q1 × 0.85
    - Final forecast floor = FY26Q1 × 0.90
  This prevents the nonsensical situation of forecasting a growing
  product below its current demand level.

  RESULT: v4.0 = 4,641 → v5.0 = 5,800 (+25%)

FLAW #4 [MAJOR] — Structural signals ignored FY26Q1 crash
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  THE PROBLEM:
  Products with a recent Q1 crash (FY26Q1 << FY25Q1) had structural
  signals based on OLD, high-demand quarters. The YoY Q2 and MA4 signals
  are backward-looking and don't know that Q1 just collapsed.

  Example (Product #4):
    FY25Q1 = 27,684 → FY26Q1 = 11,659 (dropped 58%!)
    But MA4 = 19,002 (uses FY25Q2=19180, FY25Q3=26305, FY25Q4=5887, FY26Q1=11659)
    And YoY Q2 = 20,232 (based on old Q2 growth of +5.5%)
    Neither signal reflects the current crash.

  THE FIX (v5.0):
  FY26Q1-AWARE STRUCTURAL INTELLIGENCE:
  If FY26Q1 dropped > 25% from FY25Q1:
    - Q2/Q1 ratio FC gets 60% weight (it uses CURRENT Q1 as base)
    - YoY and MA4 (backward-looking) share 40% weight
  This ensures the structural composite responds to recent demand shifts.

FLAW #5 [MAJOR] — Structural cap used max(Q1, Q2) — wrong for decline
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  THE PROBLEM:
  v4.0's structural cap for decline products used:
    cap = max(FY26Q1, FY25Q2) × 1.10
  For Product #4: max(11659, 19180) × 1.10 = 21,098 — way too high!
  The old Q2 of 19,180 inflated the cap, making it useless.

  THE FIX (v5.0):
  Cap based on Q2/Q1 ratio × FY26Q1 (if available):
    cap = max(ratio_fc, FY26Q1) × 1.15
  This uses CURRENT demand levels, not historical peaks.

FLAW #6 [MODERATE] — Q2/Q1 ratio had no cap → extreme outlier signals
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  THE PROBLEM:
  Product #14 (Switch 8P Ethernet) had Q2/Q1 ratio of 2.83 (from a
  one-time spike quarter: FY25Q2=10,291 / FY25Q1=3,638).
  Product #20 had ratio of 4.01 (seasonal anomaly).
  These extreme ratios produced absurd structural forecasts:
    #14: 6,327 × 2.83 = 17,902 → wildly inflated
    #20: 1,053 × 4.01 = 4,222 → misleading

  THE FIX (v5.0):
  Cap Q2/Q1 ratio at [0.20, 2.0]:
    - Maximum 2.0 prevents spike-quarter ratios from producing absurd signals
    - Minimum 0.20 prevents collapse-quarter ratios from zeroing out forecasts
  After cap: #14 ratio = 2.0 → 6,327 × 2.0 = 12,654 (still high but bounded)

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

  KEY LEARNINGS ACROSS ALL VERSIONS:

  1. EXPERTS > STATISTICS (for most products)
     Every version that increased expert weight on reliable products improved.
     The best forecasts came from products where experts had >80% accuracy.

  2. SIGNAL INDEPENDENCE MATTERS
     Having 7 signals sounds better than 3, but not when 5 of them are
     the same data in different packaging. Quality > quantity.

  3. TEST EVERYTHING
     v2.0's damped trend improvement (8.6pp) was discovered through holdout
     testing, not intuition. v4.0's Product #4 flaw (60% overforecast) was
     discovered through forensic auditing. Never trust a model you haven't
     stress-tested.

  4. DOMINANT EXPERTS DESERVE DOMINANT WEIGHT
     When one expert has 88% accuracy and another has 68%, the better expert
     should drive the forecast. acc^2 weighting wasn't aggressive enough.
     The dominant expert rule (80% anchor on best when gap ≥8%) was the
     single most impactful fix.

  5. RECENT DATA > HISTORICAL DATA
     When FY26Q1 crashed, backward-looking signals (MA4, YoY) became stale.
     The Q2/Q1 ratio signal — which uses CURRENT Q1 as its base — was
     the only structural signal that responded to recent reality.
